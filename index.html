Dungeons and Dragons Character Creation Guide

Welcome Your adventure awaits

Hear ye, Hear ye the time of reckoning is near. Whether you are a complete beginner or a seasoned dungeoneerer, creating a character has always been a time consuming process. This guide is designed to make character creation quick and imaginative. Whether the goblins are out of hand or a moon is on a collision course, this guide will get you ready to tackle your next adventure.

Play Style Let's start here

Do you know what you want to play as? Describe your character in a few words; write it down on some scratch paper if you like.

Examples
Happy Healer
Headstrong Lancer
Mysterious Magician
Pickpocket Rouge
To create a fun character, it is key to understand your own play style. You should be able to summarize your character's play style in a few words. If you need inspiration, try using one of your favorite fantasy characters as a model. Just be sure to check with the DM.
Each DM has their own style of game play. Ask your DM what kind of world you will be adventuring in or what other party members have decided to play as.

Ability Scores Let's roll some dice!

  • Roll 4d6
  • Remove the lowest 1
  • Total the remaining 3
  • Write down this value on some scrap paper
  • You will want to do this 6 times
Don't worry about putting these on your character sheet just yet, we will get to that later.

Your character has 6 base stats <7>:
Stat Abbr Use
Strength (STR) Swinging a sword, lifting something Heavy
Dexterity (DEX) Picking a lock, shooting an arrow
Constitution (CON) Tenacity, resistance to poisoning
Intelligence (INT) Reading, how many things you know
Wisdom (WIS) Tracking, sensing someones motive
Charisma (CHA) Diplomacy, how well you are with people
Some DM's handle rolling stat scores differently. Check if they are using alternative methods.

Appearance and Traits Damn! I look good

Hopefully by now you have a clear idea of your character's play style, and maybe have some back story ideas.

Fill out the fields at the top of your character sheet:

  • Character Name
  • Player Name
  • Class and your Level (This is '1')
  • Race
  • Size
  • Gender
  • Alignment
  • Religion/Patron Deity
  • Height
  • Weight
  • Looks
  • Additional information for any of these can be found on page <103>

You will need everything in bold for other stats and game play.

Size is how big your character is in relation to everything else. Being bigger gives you better armor and damage, but being smaller makes you more dexterous. <134>

Alignment is how good or evil you are, in combination to the likelihood you will break the law. <104>

Religion is mostly used for clerics, but it can impact your roleplay if you adhere to your characters beliefs. <106>

DM's usually place restrictions over your alignment. Make sure you have permission if playing any of the extremes.
Character names are based off of their race. Have fun here with your lore.

Stats Oh no... Maths

Your stats are what brings mechanics into the game. While there are a lot of them, most of it is repeated information (Modifiers) and you shouldn't have to do anything beyond basic arithmetic.

This next part will be broken into steps as there is a lot to cover.

1. Place and Sort your Base Stats

Take the numbers you wrote down earlier, then sort them into your classes preferred stats. Then add any racial stat modifications you may have.

Place this total in the "Base Score + Racial Mod." field.

2. Write down Ability Modifiers

Next we will write down your Ability Modifiers from the following table in the Modifier Fields to the right of your Base Stats.

Ability Modifiers
Your stat Modifier
1 -5
2 - 3 -4
4 - 5 -3
6 - 7 -2
8 - 9 -1
10 - 11  0
12 - 13 +1
14 - 15 +2
16 - 17 +3
18 - 19 +4
20 - 21 +5

Your ability modifier is your working number for each ability. It is what you will add to your rolls and your other stats. <7>

3. Base Attack Bonus

Under your combat options, write down your Base Attack Bonus, this is dependent on your class and level.

Each class has a standing which is either good, poor, or average. Depending on your standing, as you level your bonus will vary according to this table found on page <22>.

Your Base Attack Bonus is a bonus to rolling attacks. When rolling, you add your Base Stat modifier, Size modifiers, Racial modifiers, miscellaneous modifiers, then finally your Base Attack Bonus. This will depend on your class and level. <22>
There is a condensed table for this alongside your Saving Throws within your class pages in the Players Handbook. I recommend you bookmark it.

4. Health Points

Health points (HP) represents your health. Each class has its own hit die. Each time you level, you will gain a roll of this hit die plus your constitution modifier.

For level 1, you will just take the maximum your hit die can roll, then add your CON for your starting health.

Hit Die for each basic class <23>:
Die Class(s)
d4 Sorcerer, Wizard
d6 Bard, Rouge
d8 Cleric, Druid, Monk, Ranger
d10 Fighter, Paladin
d12 Barbarian
Note:
Should you roll a total of 0 or less after constitution modifier, you will gain 1 Hp regardless.

It goes without saying, when your HP hits 0 your character is in the process of dying†. More info on HP <23> and <136>.

† Should this happen see necromancy <171>

I recommend that you draw a box over your max Hp.

Keep a current count below this. When in battle, minus from the current count, when you get healed, either add to the current count or erase it entirely. When you level, just update your max Hp Box.

Armor and Weapons I swing my sword... again

Next we will be equipping your character <111>.

It is important to note that every DM does this differently. Check with yours to see what you can choose for armor and weapons.

Weapons

All basic weapons are listed on a table on pages <116-117>.

When picking a weapon, make sure to check with what your class prefers. Then write down the following for each weapon you are allowed to carry:

  • Name
  • Attack bonus
    • Ranged uses DEX
    • Melee uses STR
  • Damage
    • Write down the respectful die for your size
  • Critical modifier
  • Range
  • Type

You might not have access to Martial or Exotic Weapons

Armor

Armor is what keeps your character safe and is essential to avoid getting mauled on your first encounter. We will pick armor, then fill in the fields to find your total Armor Class (AC).

All basic armor is listed in a table on page <116-117>

Choose your armor, then write it down on the bottom of your character sheet. Make sure to include the following:

  • Name
  • Max Dex
  • Check Penalty
  • Weight
  • If you have a shield, add it too
Careful as Medium and Heavy armor will reduce your Speed. Make sure to update this is you are planning on wearing this kind of armor.
The Revised Character sheet has fields for all of these things.

Next, we will calculate your AC.

Total your armor bonus, shield bonus, DEX (but not exceeding your armors max DEX), size modifier, any natural armor your race or class may have, deflection modifier, then anything miscellaneous outstanding.
Then, add 10 to your total

Finally, we will calculate your Touch AC, and Flat-Foooted AC

Touch AC is your current AC minus your Armor Bonus

Flat-Footed AC is your current AC minus your DEX modifier

Skills I didn't know that I didn't know that

Next we will place our skills. This is the most customizable part of creating a character I feel.

First, we will need to know your total number of skill points. This is calculated from your intelligence.

The following table has all of the skill point formulas for each class:

Calculate Skill Points
(4 + INT) x 4 Barbarian
(4 + INT) x 4 Bard
(2 + INT) x 4 Cleric
(4 + INT) x 4 Druid
(2 + INT) x 4 Fighter
(4 + INT) x 4 Monk
(2 + INT) x 4 Paladin
(4 + INT) x 4 Ranger
(8 + INT) x 4 Rouge
(2 + INT) x 4 Sorcerer
(2 + INT) x 4 Wizard
As you level, you will gain additional points, and your maximum rank will inrease.

Next, check and see what skills your class has available to them at level one.

Color in the boxes to the left of the skills that your class provides.

Any ability that already has a black diamond next to it means that you can rank this up despite your class not supporting it.

At level 1 you can spend a maximum of 4 skill points (ranks) per skill. Your total number of ranks must not exceed the total number of skill points you calculated earlier.

Finally, add the required ability modifier for each skill, and any miscellaneous modifiers you might have (usually feats). Total all of these to get your skill modifier for that particular skill.

Feats Yes, I am legendary

At last, Feats. The number of starting feats you get at level 1 depends on your Class and Race. Check those pages to see how many you are allowed to have.

In the book, on pages <90-91> is a table of all of the available basic feats.

Write down what you have chosen in the feats section, and update any stats they might have adjusted.

Feats are abilities that are unique to your character, think of them as unlockable talents. Some feats require a certain amount of stats or other feats as prerequisites.

For example, if you are particularly good at shooting a bow, you can do more damage from point blank. This is a feat.

Final Thoughts Good luck and God Speed!

Now you are finally ready to take to the open roads. Your adventure awaits. Good luck adventurer and may your path be true.

Now that you have completed the guide, have your DM review your character sheet to see if there is anything else they would like you to add.
